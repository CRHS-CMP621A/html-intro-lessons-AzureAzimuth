4 Historical Cybersecurity Events (Web)

The LinkedIn Data Breach (2021)

The LinkedIn Data Breach occured in 2021, when attackers exploited the company's API in order to collect user data. They then sold the scraped dataset, which contained names, emails, phone numbers and location details. 92% of LinkedIn's user base was impacted by the breach.

Yahoo Breaches (2013-2014)

The Yahoo data breaches occured in years 2013 and 2014. Attackes did this by manipulating web cookies in order to authenticate as other users without the need of passwords. In 2014, the attacker woud copy a backup of yahoo's account databse that contained over 500 million accounts. To this day, this incident remains as one of Yahoo's biggest data breaches in its lifetime.

Equifax Breach (2017)

A vulnerability in Apache Struts allowed attackers to manipulate web forms to breach systems in 2017, exposing the personal information of 147.9 million Americans and millions in the UK and Canada. This incident caused damages totalling to $425 million settlements.

Heartland Payment Systems (2008)

Russian hackers would use an SQL Injection attack through a webform, gaining initial access to the company's corporate network. This would lead to over 130 million credit/debit card numbers becoming compromised as data was intercepted in transit.
